How to use this site:

Select a time range on the frequency chart (bottom left) to show asteroids that came their nearest to Earth at that time of year. The vertical bars next to that are used to filter by diameter and velocity. Clicking an asteroid on the orbit chart (top left) will show you details in the info panel (right of this text), and hovering over a data node on any chart will highlight that asteroid on all of the charts at once. The horizontal positioning of items in the orbit chart is proportional to each NEO's actual approach distance to Earth's surface. Vertical spacing is only used to separate markers, and radius is scaled by about 0.17 kilometers per pixel, with a minimum radius of 3 pixels for visibility.
