How to use this site:

This website uses a sample set of data from NASA's NeoWs API, described at https://api.nasa.gov, to show patterns in near-earth objects (NEOs), which include asteroids and icy bodies, that have had near approaches with Earth. The timeline below shows frequencies of approach by month from 2019 to 2022.

Select a time range on the frequency chart (bottom left) to show asteroids that came their nearest to Earth at that time of year. The vertical bars next to that are used to filter by diameter and velocity. Clicking an asteroid on the orbit chart (top left) will show you details in the info panel (right of this text), and hovering over a data node on any chart will highlight that asteroid on all of the charts at once.

The horizontal positioning of items in the orbit chart is proportional to each NEO's actual approach distance to Earth's surface. Vertical spacing is only used to separate markers, and radius is scaled by about 0.17 kilometers per pixel, with a minimum radius of 3 pixels for visibility. Marker radius does NOT represent diameter proportional to Earth's because that would make the markers too small to see. The largest NEO in the dataset has a median estimated diameter of 5.937 KM and the smallest NEO has an estimated diameter of 0.017 KM.
